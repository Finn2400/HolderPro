# HolderPro community conduct

HolderPro is built in public by people with different backgrounds, abilities,
and levels of experience. Participation must remain safe, useful, and focused
on improving the project.

## Expected conduct

Participants should:

- discuss ideas and code without attacking the person presenting them;
- give specific, actionable feedback and receive it in good faith;
- make room for newcomers to ask basic questions and learn;
- respect names, pronouns, privacy, accessibility needs, and personal
  boundaries;
- disclose conflicts that could affect a project decision; and
- stop an interaction when a participant or moderator asks them to stop.

## Unacceptable conduct

HolderPro community spaces do not permit harassment, threats, stalking,
discriminatory abuse, sexualized attention, deliberate humiliation, repeated
disruption, publication of private information, impersonation, or retaliation
against someone who reports a concern. Spam, malware, knowingly unsafe release
advice, and attempts to obtain user models or credentials deceptively are also
prohibited.

Disagreement, criticism, and firm technical review are allowed. They must stay
relevant to the work and must not become personal abuse.

## Scope

This policy applies in HolderPro repositories, issues, pull requests,
discussions, release channels, and events, as well as when someone is publicly
representing the project.

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns confidentially through HolderPro's
[private report form](https://github.com/Finn2400/HolderPro/security/advisories/new)
with **Conduct report** as the title. Do not include a user model or unrelated
private data. The current maintainer reviews reports; if the report concerns
that maintainer, ask a trusted GitHub support or university/community contact to
preserve the report while an independent reviewer is identified.

## Response

Maintainers will consider the available evidence, the safety of affected
people, intent, impact, prior behavior, and willingness to repair harm. A
response may include clarification, a private warning, removal of content,
temporary participation limits, or a permanent ban. Reports and private details
will be shared only as needed to investigate or protect participants.

No contributor is entitled to continued access to project-managed spaces when
their conduct makes those spaces unsafe or prevents productive collaboration.
